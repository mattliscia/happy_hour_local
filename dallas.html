City Guide

Dallas

Rooftop cocktails, steakhouse happy hours, and a nightlife scene that runs from polished Uptown to gritty Deep Ellum. Dallas does it bigger.

See Live Deals ↓

Dallas has a reputation for being all flash and no substance—and honestly, the Uptown happy hour scene can feel that way sometimes. But dig a little deeper and you'll find one of the most rewarding drinking cities in Texas. The steakhouse happy hours alone are worth the trip: we're talking $12 prime cuts and $8 old fashioneds at places where dinner would cost you triple digits. Deep Ellum brings the grit and creativity that Austin gets all the credit for. And the rooftop bar scene? Unmatched in the state. Dallas happy hours reward the curious drinker who's willing to look beyond the bottle-service crowd and find the spots where the bartenders actually care about what's in your glass.

Happy Hours & Drink Deals

Best Overall Happy Hours

Dallas happy hours tend to run generous—many spots do 3–7 PM with significant discounts on premium cocktails and wine. The Design District and Trinity Groves area have some of the best value, with newer restaurants competing hard for the after-work crowd. Several spots in these areas offer $6 craft cocktails and half-price wine that would be double at dinner.

Rooftop Specials

This is Dallas's calling card. The skyline views from the rooftop bars in Uptown and downtown are legitimately stunning, and the happy hour deals make them accessible. Multiple rooftop spots run weekday specials with $7–9 cocktails and $5 beer. The trick is going during actual happy hour—prices jump significantly after 7 PM and on weekends. Best sunset views face west toward the Kessler Park hills.

Steakhouse Happy Hours

This is Dallas's secret weapon. Several high-end steakhouses run bar-only happy hours with steeply discounted food and drinks. You can eat a $60 steak dinner for under $25 if you sit at the bar between 4–6 PM. The Harwood District and downtown locations tend to have the best programs. Always sit at the bar—the happy hour menu often isn't available at tables.

Sports Bar Scene

Dallas is a sports town, and the sports bar happy hours reflect it. Cowboys, Mavs, Rangers, Stars—there's always a game on and always a deal attached to it. Henderson Avenue and Lower Greenville have the best concentration of sports bars with game-day specials. Several spots do $3 domestics and half-price wings during any Dallas team game.

Dive Bar Finds

Yes, Dallas has dive bars. The best ones are in East Dallas, along Lowest Greenville, and scattered through Oak Cliff. These are the places where your beer costs $3, the jukebox actually has good music, and nobody is trying to impress anyone. The further you get from Uptown, the better the dives get.

Food & Dining Deals

Food Happy Hours

Dallas restaurants compete hard on happy hour food menus. Trinity Groves—the restaurant incubator district across the Margaret Hunt Hill Bridge—is ground zero for creative happy hour bites at steep discounts. Several places in Bishop Arts do small plates for $4–6 during happy hour that rival their dinner menu quality.

Tex-Mex & Margaritas

Every Dallas neighborhood has a Tex-Mex spot doing frozen margarita specials. The Oak Lawn and Lakewood areas have some of the most beloved Tex-Mex joints in the city, many with generous happy hour programs—$5 house margs and half-price queso are standard. Skip the chains and find the family-owned spots; the salsa is always better.

Late Night Eats

Deep Ellum is the undisputed late-night food king. Tacos, pizza, ramen, and burgers all available until 2 AM or later on weekends. Lower Greenville has a solid late-night scene too, with several spots doing post-10 PM food specials to catch the bar crowd. The taco joints in Oak Cliff stay open late and are worth the rideshare.

Nightlife & Events

Key Entertainment Districts

Deep Ellum: The creative heart of Dallas nightlife. Live music venues, craft cocktail bars, breweries, and street art. This is where you go when you want an actual experience, not just a drink. Uptown: Polished, trendy, and full of beautiful people. Great rooftop bars and upscale lounges. Can feel a bit scene-y but the venues are genuinely nice. Lower Greenville: The sweet spot between Deep Ellum grit and Uptown polish. Neighborhood bars, good food, and a walkable strip that doesn't require bottle service. Bishop Arts: Oak Cliff's gem. Indie shops, art galleries, and small bars with personality. More laid back, very walkable, and feels like a different city from Uptown.

Weekly Events

Tuesday is trivia night city-wide. Wednesday is industry night in Deep Ellum—bartenders and servers flood the district and prices drop. Thursday is the unofficial start to the weekend in Uptown. Multiple Deep Ellum venues do free live shows on weeknights that rival paid concerts in other cities.

Live Music & Entertainment

Deep Ellum's venues book everything from punk to R&B to country. The club scene in the Design District draws big-name DJs. Dallas's comedy scene has gotten surprisingly good, with multiple clubs doing affordable weeknight shows. The Truck Yard concept—outdoor bars with live music and food trucks—has been a game-changer for casual entertainment.

Daytime Activities

Day Drinking Spots

The Katy Trail is Dallas's version of Austin's Lady Bird Lake, and the bars along it cater to the post-run/bike crowd with afternoon specials. White Rock Lake is gorgeous for a morning activity followed by a long lunch at one of the Lakewood spots nearby. The brewery scene in the Design District and Deep Ellum is great for weekend afternoon hopping.

Outdoor Activities

Klyde Warren Park downtown is a deck park over a highway that hosts free fitness classes, food trucks, and events. White Rock Lake is a 9-mile loop perfect for running or biking. The Trinity River trails are expanding and offer a surprisingly natural escape from the city. The Dallas Arboretum is one of the most beautiful green spaces in Texas.

Cultural Attractions

The Dallas Arts District is the largest contiguous urban arts district in the country. The Nasher Sculpture Center, Dallas Museum of Art (free general admission), and the Perot Museum are all worth your time. Dealey Plaza and the Sixth Floor Museum are obviously significant. The Bishop Arts District has the best gallery-hopping in the city.

Neighborhood Guide

Deep Ellum

Creative & Electric

The cultural engine of Dallas. Street murals, live music every night, and bars that range from cocktail dens to punk dives. Best nightlife in the city, hands down. Getting more polished but hasn't lost its soul yet.

Uptown

Polished & Social

The see-and-be-seen neighborhood. Rooftop bars, upscale restaurants, and a young professional crowd. Great happy hours at premium spots. Can feel pretentious but the views and venues are legitimately good.

Bishop Arts (Oak Cliff)

Eclectic & Charming

Dallas's most walkable neighborhood with the most personality. Independent shops, art galleries, mezcal bars, and taquerias. Feels like a small town within the city. Growing fast but still genuine.

Lower Greenville

Neighborhood & Balanced

The Goldilocks zone of Dallas nightlife. Not too fancy, not too gritty, just right. Great sports bars, solid restaurants, and a walkable strip with enough variety to spend a whole evening without getting bored.

Design District

Modern & Emerging

Showrooms by day, breweries and cocktail bars by night. The Design District is Dallas's newest drinking destination with some of the most architecturally interesting bar spaces in the city. Great brewery trail.

Trinity Groves

Foodie & Experimental

A restaurant incubator district with some of the most creative food concepts in Dallas. Happy hour here means sampling cutting-edge restaurants at happy hour prices. The bridge views are a bonus.
